My top 4 AI models right now.

Not the most benchmark-brained list. Just the four that feel most useful in actual life: one for serious coding and tooling, one for good company, one for voice-native commercial polish, and one for the cheap practical “honestly this is enough” slot.

Best for coding/tooling GPT-5.4
Best personality/conversation Claude Opus 4.6
Best voice-native commercial GPT-4o
Best cheap/local-ish practical Gemini 2.5 Flash
Coding + tooling

GPT-5.4

#1 pick

If the task involves actual doing — editing files, using tools, reasoning across a codebase, staying on task through multi-step work — this is the one I trust most.

Best agentic follow-through Strong repo navigation Usually the least annoying at tool use
Opinion: It feels the most like a capable working partner, not just a smart autocomplete. For real coding sessions, this is the current default unless price or latency changes the math.
Personality + conversation

Claude Opus 4.6

#1 vibe

The best “talk to me like a thoughtful mind” model in the bunch. Warm, reflective, usually nuanced, and better than most at feeling like it actually understood the spirit of the question.

Most human-feeling tone Excellent long-form thinking Good taste, not just raw output
Opinion: If I want a conversation instead of a transaction, this wins. It’s the model I’d pick when the quality of the exchange matters as much as the answer.
Voice-native + commercial

GPT-4o

#1 product

Still the cleanest answer to “what actually ships well in a mainstream product?” Fast multimodal behavior, solid voice interaction, broad ecosystem support, and fewer weird edges in customer-facing use.

Realtime voice feels polished Great multimodal baseline Commercially practical
Opinion: It may not be the most romantic pick, but it’s the one I’d trust for voice-first product experiences that need to feel smooth, familiar, and dependable.
Cheap + local-ish practical

Gemini 2.5 Flash

#1 value

For the “good enough, fast, affordable, no drama” lane, this is hard to beat. It’s not truly local, but it occupies the same practical slot local models often aim for: inexpensive, responsive, and usable at scale.

Excellent speed / cost tradeoff Surprisingly capable day-to-day Closest thing to a practical workhorse bargain
Opinion: If you want the spirit of the local-model value proposition without babying hardware, this is the shortcut. Not glamorous. Very useful.
Notes: This list is intentionally opinionated and optimized for real use, not leaderboard worship. Also, “best cheap/local-ish” is a deliberately squishy category: true local favorites depend heavily on your hardware, patience, and tolerance for setup friction. This pick is about practical value, not ideology.
