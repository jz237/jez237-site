Twombly's Red Sentinel

A narrow red column with a spring glow, made even better by the gold-dusted green behind it.

Leafing out in red

This is that sharp early maple stage where the new growth looks almost lit from inside, bright red at the tips, deepening lower down, and already reading in the upright disciplined form this cultivar is known for.

spring leaf-out upright columnar form great contrast planting
April 12, 2026

Red against gold dust

The Twombly’s Red Sentinel is leafing out in that sharp spring red that almost looks backlit, bright at the tips, deeper burgundy through the older leaves, and still narrow and upright the way this cultivar should be. Even young, it already has that vertical, disciplined silhouette that makes it feel more architectural than soft.
Fine-cut red foliage in front, gold-flecked evergreen behind it, one of those accidental color pairings that looks completely intentional.
  • strong spring leaf-out
  • bright red new growth at the tips
  • narrow upright structure is already reading clearly
  • excellent contrast with the gold dust aucuba behind it
