Seasonal Storytelling

Plant Progress

A warm, month-by-month journal for the plants that make the yard feel alive.

How it should feel

Not just a gallery. More like a living field journal, where each plant gets its own little chapter and every two weeks or so becomes part of the season’s story.

bud break first bloom leaf color stress + recovery late-season glow

Update rhythm

Each plant page is meant to become a real timeline, one page, many dated entries, added week by week or every two weeks as the season moves.

Best practice: keep adding entries to the same plant page instead of making a new page for every photo.

Suggested photo path: photos/garden/plants/<slug>/YYYY/MM/

Suggested note fields: date, caption, what changed, what you did, what to watch next.

How this will grow

The goal is to keep Plant Progress readable even when the photo count gets large. Best entries become part of a longer seasonal story instead of floating around as one-offs.

Plant timelines

  • one plant page, many dated entries
  • best for maples, hydrangeas, shrubs, vines, and long-season perennials
  • lets you actually watch change instead of just storing pictures

Bed journals

  • track full beds when multiple plants matter together
  • save layout ideas, revisions, and before-and-after plans
  • good for side-yard beds and mixed borders

Seasonal archive later

  • Spring, Summer, and Fall views can come later
  • lets the site scale without turning messy
  • keeps the strongest entries easy to find
