Plant Progress · Sambucus racemosa 'Lemony Lace'

Lemony Lace Elderberry

A bright cut-leaf elderberry that reads almost electric in spring, airy in texture but surprisingly assertive once the light hits it.

Fine texture, loud color

Lemony Lace has that unusual combination of being visually soft and impossible to ignore. The foliage is finely cut and almost feathery, but the chartreuse-yellow color makes it stand out like a lit object in the middle of the brick and mulch.

cut-leaf shrub chartreuse foliage spring structure
April 17, 2026

Bright against the brick

The Lemony Lace elderberry is already doing what it does best here, throwing bright feathery foliage into full sun so hard it almost glows against the brick-edged bed. The white flower clusters are starting to show too, which gives the whole plant a second texture on top of the cut foliage and makes it feel less like filler and more like a real focal shrub.
It looks airy from a distance, but the color gives it real weight.
  • bright chartreuse-yellow spring foliage is fully readable already
  • finely cut leaves give the shrub a soft feathery texture
  • white flower clusters are beginning to show
  • strong contrast against the brick surround and darker background fence
April 27, 2026

Feathery and fully lit

The Lemony Lace elderberry is carrying a much fuller shape now, with fine chartreuse foliage layered from the base all the way up through the staked central stem. Against the brick and weathered wood, the plant reads almost fern-like, but with enough height and color to hold its own as a real focal shrub.
It has the lightness of a fern, but the presence of a small tree in training.
  • cutleaf chartreuse foliage is full and bright across the whole plant
  • new tip growth is extending upward and outward
  • staked central form is giving the young shrub a clear vertical line
  • brick, mulch, and raised beds make the yellow foliage stand out strongly
