March 29, 2026

Before the show

A quieter look at the dwarf peach before the pink show started, bare branching still exposed, buds swelling along the stems, and just enough movement to hint that bloom was close. This is the useful stage to remember later, because once the tree explodes into flower it’s easy to forget how quickly it turned.
All structure and promise, the kind of in-between moment that only feels dramatic after you’ve seen what comes next.
  • buds swelling along the branches
  • tree still mostly bare
  • pre-bloom structure easy to see
  • Pugster behind it still mostly acting as quiet background structure
April 12, 2026

Full spring pink

The dwarf peach is putting on a real spring show here, deep pink bloom clustered all along the branches while the first narrow green leaves start to slip out between the flowers. It still has that young upright shape, but the bloom is already strong enough to make it feel established, not tentative.
Small tree, big entrance. The kind of spring color that completely changes the mood of a corner.
  • peak to near-peak bloom
  • early leaf-out underway
  • strong flowering across the canopy
  • nice contrast with the Pugster butterfly bush behind it
